Consult Your Doctor

We're here for comfort — but your doctor is here for care. Here's how to make the most of every appointment.

Your doctor is your partner

Cancer treatment is a journey, and your doctor walks it with you. But appointments can feel overwhelming — too fast, too technical, too emotional.

Here are some gentle guides to help you prepare, ask, and understand.

Helpful Guides

📋

Before Your Appointment

Write down your symptoms, questions, and concerns. Bring someone with you if you can — two ears are better than one.

🗣️

Questions to Ask

What are my treatment options? What side effects should I expect? What can I do to feel better day-to-day? It's okay to ask anything.

📝

After Your Appointment

Write down what you remember. Share notes with family. Don't Google symptoms at 2am — call your doctor instead.

Topics Worth Discussing

Ask about what side effects to expect, how to manage them, and when to call. Knowing what's coming helps reduce anxiety. Your care team may have solutions you haven't heard of.

Taste changes, nausea, and appetite loss are common. Ask about foods that help, hydration tips, and whether supplements would be beneficial. Small meals, warm soups, and ginger chai often work wonders.

Don't hesitate to ask for a referral to a counsellor or psychologist. Many hospitals have support services. Visit our Mental Health page for more resources.

Fever above 100.4°F, persistent vomiting, unusual bleeding, or any sudden change — these are reasons to call immediately. It's always better to ask than to worry alone.

Seeking a second opinion is completely okay and many doctors encourage it. It's about finding the treatment approach that feels right for you and your family.
