Mental Health Matters

The journey isn't just physical. We see the emotional weight too.

It's okay to not be okay

Cancer affects more than the body. It touches the mind, the heart, the spirit — of the patient and of everyone around them.

We believe mental health care should be part of every treatment journey. Not as an afterthought, but as a foundation.

Small Steps That Help

A cancer diagnosis involves loss — loss of normalcy, loss of certainty, sometimes loss of identity. Grieving these losses isn't weakness. It's the beginning of healing.

Support groups, therapists specializing in oncology, or even online communities can provide a space where you don't have to explain yourself. Sometimes just being heard is medicine.

A morning cup of chai. An evening walk. A 5-minute breathing exercise. Small rituals anchor us when everything else feels chaotic. Visit our Rituals page for ideas.

Every person's experience with cancer is different. What works for one may not work for another. Honour your own pace. There's no "right way" to go through this.

If anxiety or depression is affecting your daily life, please reach out to a mental health professional. It's one of the bravest things you can do. Visit our Consult Your Doctor page for guidance.
